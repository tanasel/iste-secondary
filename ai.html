RESPONSIBLE AI × ISTE

Using AI well — and safely — in secondary lessons

AI doesn't sit outside the ISTE Standards — it runs straight through several of them. Used thoughtfully it strengthens research, creativity and citizenship. The job is to keep the pedagogy first and the privacy watertight.

ISTE alignment

Where AI maps to the standards

Knowledge Constructor

Students evaluate AI output for accuracy and bias; AI as a starting point to interrogate, never the final word.

Creative Communicator

AI to draft, translate or remix — always disclosed and edited, with the student's own thinking on top.

Digital Citizen

The ethics: bias, attribution, data, and what it means to use AI honestly in assessed work.

Empowered Learner

AI as a patient tutor and feedback partner that adapts to the learner.

Privacy first — non-negotiables GDPR

  • Never put student personal data (names, photos, SEN/behaviour notes, grades tied to names) into a public/consumer AI tool.
  • Prefer school-approved, data-protected tools; check where data is processed and stored.
  • Anonymise before you analyse — strip identifying details first.
  • Teacher-facing AI (e.g. lesson and resource generation) is the safest entry point; student-facing rollout needs approval and guardrails.
Classroom judgement

Good practice vs risk

Do

  • Model your prompts openly.
  • Teach students to check AI claims.
  • Set clear rules for assessed work.

Don't

  • Ban it and pretend it's gone.
  • Let it replace thinking.
  • Feed it personal data.

The aim isn't to chase every tool — it's to help colleagues adopt AI realistically, with judgement, so it actually improves teaching.
